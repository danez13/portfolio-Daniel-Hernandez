summary

A 3D rendering of the popular snake game originally created in 1997 for Nokia devices. This 3D version of the game uses computer graphics and visualization techniques to display, interact with, and view the surrounding world.

The world is set in a cube with multicolored walls. Bunnies are displayed in random positions, scattered across the map. The snake, which is initially in a fixed position, can be moved across the map in order to eat the bunnies. Both the snake and the bunnies have options to change their appearance; anything from the snake’s color to the bunnies’ size, or vice versa. Other options include the snake’s speed as it traverses through the cube or the amount of bunnies it will face during the game.

In addition, keyboard inputs exist to rotate the scene on different axes, panning the camera in different directions, zooming in and out, and lastly providing third person perspective and first person perspective options. In third person perspective, the bunnies are shaded darker depending on their distance from the camera, while in first person perspective the boundary of the world is viewed by the inside of the cube the snake is in.

why?

Originally slated as a simple 3D recreation of Snake inspired by my own experience playing Snake at age 3 on my dad’s old Nokia phone, where an 8-bit version of the game was installed.

This game was created as a project in my Computer Graphics course, where we developed the features and aspects of the game using FreeGLUT, C++, and graphic techniques like ray tracing, curve approximation, and shape creation.
